# Lab 2: Daily Workday Assistant

### Estimated Duration: 45 Minutes

## Overview
In this hands-on lab, you will use Copilot Cowork to build a personalized daily workday assistant that reads your calendar, summarizes important emails, and extracts tasks and follow-ups automatically - all from a single prompt. Copilot Cowork is the agentic workspace inside Microsoft 365 Copilot: instead of one question and one answer, it takes multi-step actions across your apps, reading your Outlook calendar, searching your inbox, scanning Teams messages, and creating tasks in Microsoft To Do within a single conversation. As you work through this lab, watch the Workspace panel on the right, where Cowork lists the Steps it is completing and the Skills it activates for each task - Daily Briefing, Calendar Management, Email Triage, and Task Extraction. This lab builds on the sample emails and calendar events created in Lab 1.

## Objectives
In this lab, you will perform the following:

- Task 1: Prepare a Personalized Workday Briefing
- Task 2: Review Meetings and Calendar Priorities
- Task 3: Summarize Important Emails and Pending Actions
- Task 4: Extract Tasks and Follow-up Items

## Task 1: Prepare a Personalized Workday Briefing

In this task, you will use Cowork's Daily Briefing skill to pull together everything you need to start your day - meetings, priority emails, and recent commitments - in a single multi-source prompt.

1. Click on **New task** in the left navigation panel.

    ![](media/86.png)

2. Enter the following prompt **(1)** and click on **Send (2)** button:

   ```
   Create my morning briefing for today. Include:

   1. My meetings today with a one-line purpose for each.
   2. The three most important unread emails and why they matter.
   3. Any deadlines or commitments mentioned in email or Teams in the last 48 hours.

   Format it as a short brief that I can read in under two minutes.
   ```

   ![](media/87.png)

3. After submitting the prompt, you can see that Cowork has started **processing** the request.

   ![](media/88.png)

4. Review the **Morning Briefing** generated by Cowork.

   ![](media/89.png)

   >**Note**: It is normal if some information is missing or not entirely accurate. Copilot Cowork can only use the data available in your Microsoft 365 environment. The next step refines the prompt to improve the quality and relevance of the results.

5. Enter the below follow-up prompt **(1)** and click on **Send (2)** button:

   ```
   Update my morning briefing with the following preferences:

   - Always list emails from my manager first.
   - Ignore newsletters and promotional emails.
   ```

   ![](media/90.png)

   >**Note**: Personalization rules help Copilot Cowork tailor its responses to your work style and priorities. Adding clear preferences - such as prioritizing messages from your manager or excluding low-value emails - results in more relevant and actionable briefings.

6. Review the confirmation from Cowork. It saves two personalization rules - **manager-first** (anything from your manager appears at the top of every section) and **ignore newsletters** (promotional email is dropped entirely) - and confirms they will apply automatically to all future briefings.

    ![](media/91.png)

## Task 2: Review Meetings and Calendar Priorities

In this task, you will use Cowork's Calendar Management skill to scan your full week, flag meetings that are missing agendas or overlapping, and recommend which ones to prioritize, shorten, reschedule, or decline.

1. Enter the following prompt **(1)** and click on **Send (2)** button:

   ```
   Review my calendar for the rest of this week.

   - Identify meetings that do not have an agenda or description.
   - Highlight any scheduling conflicts or overlapping meetings.
   - Recommend which meetings I should prioritize, shorten, reschedule, or decline.
   - Explain the reasoning behind each recommendation.
   ```

   ![](media/92.png)

   >**Note**: Calendar Management only analyzes your calendar - it will not create, modify, reschedule, or delete meetings unless you explicitly instruct Cowork to do so.

2. After submitting the prompt, you can see that Cowork has started **processing** the request.

   ![](media/93.png)

3. Review the output generated by Cowork after analyzing your calendar in detail.

   ![](media/94.png)

## Task 3: Summarize Important Emails and Pending Actions

In this task, you will use Cowork's Email Triage skill to sort your last three days of email into **Needs My Reply**, **Waiting on Others**, and **FYI Only**, with a suggested response angle for anything that needs your attention.

1. Enter the following prompt **(1)** and click on **Send (2)** button:

   ```
   Review my inbox from the last three days.

   - Group my messages into the following categories:
   - Needs My Reply
   - Waiting on Others
   - FYI Only
   - For each email in **Needs My Reply**, suggest a one-line response angle to help me get started.
   ```

   ![](media/95.png)

   >**Note**: Copilot Cowork does not send emails on your behalf unless you explicitly instruct it to - it only reads, summarizes, and drafts responses for your review.

2. After submitting the prompt, you can see that Cowork has started **processing** the request.

   ![](media/96.png)

3. Review how Cowork has categorized all the emails into **Needs My Reply**, **Waiting on Others**, and **FYI Only** in the generated output.

   ![](media/97.png)

   ![](media/98.png)

4. Enter the below follow-up prompt **(1)** and click on **Send (2)** button:

   ```
   Treat the "HR / Learning & Development" email as Needs My Reply instead of its current category.
   ```

   ![](media/99.png)

5. Review the output generated by Cowork, where it has moved the "HR / Learning & Development" email under the **Needs My Reply** category.

   ![](media/100.png)

## Task 4: Extract Tasks and Follow-up Items

In this task, you will use Cowork's Task Extraction skill to find the commitments buried in your email and Teams conversations - deadlines you agreed to, replies you promised - and turn them into tasks with due dates in Microsoft To Do.

1. Enter the following prompt **(1)** and click on **Send (2)** button:

   ```
   Review my email and Microsoft Teams messages from this week.

   - Extract every task or commitment assigned to me.
   - Create a task for each item that includes a due date when one is specified.
   - List any tasks where the due date is unclear so I can confirm it before the task is created.
   ```

   ![](media/101.png)

   >**Note**: Cowork only creates a task when it has enough information - if a due date or owner is unclear, it asks you to confirm before creating the task.

2. After submitting the prompt, you can see that Cowork has started **processing** the request.

   ![](media/102.png)

3. Review the output generated by Cowork, with tasks created for clear due dates and a separate list for tasks with unclear due dates.

   ![](media/103.png)

   ![](media/104.png)

4. Enter the below follow-up prompt **(1)** and click on **Send (2)** button:

   ```
   Set due dates for tasks 1 and 2 as follows:

   - Task 1: Due tomorrow at 5:00 PM.
   - Task 2: Due this Friday at 3:00 PM.
   ```

   ![](media/105.png)

5. Review the output generated by Cowork confirming that both tasks' due dates have been updated.

   ![](media/106.png)
